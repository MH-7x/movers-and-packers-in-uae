## Tips for Preparing Items for Self Storage

A few minutes of preparation goes a long way in keeping your belongings in good shape:

**Clean everything before storing.** Wipe down furniture, wash clothing, and empty kitchen appliances. Dirt and food residue attract pests, even in controlled environments.

**Disassemble large furniture.** Beds, desks, and shelving units take up less space when broken down. Keep all screws and hardware in labeled bags taped to the item.

**Use uniform box sizes.** Same-size boxes stack neatly and make better use of the unit space. Avoid garbage bags — they offer no protection and collapse under weight.

**Label every box.** Mark the contents and the room it belongs to. When you retrieve items months later, you will not remember what is in "Box 14."

**Do not vacuum-seal clothing long-term.** Vacuum bags are fine for short-term storage, but over months they can permanently crease fabrics. Use breathable garment bags instead.

**Stand mattresses upright.** If your unit allows it, standing a mattress on its side saves floor space. Wrap it in a mattress cover first.

If you do not want to handle preparation yourself, our team will do it for you as part of the packing service.

## Why Furniture Needs Special Storage in the UAE

The UAE climate is one of the harshest environments for furniture. Summer temperatures exceed 50°C. Humidity can swing from 10% in the desert to 90% near the coast within the same week. Here is what that does to unprotected furniture:

**Wooden furniture** expands and contracts with temperature changes, causing joints to loosen, veneer to peel, and surfaces to crack.

**Leather sofas and chairs** dry out and crack in extreme heat, or develop mold in high humidity.

**Fabric upholstery** absorbs moisture and becomes a breeding ground for dust mites and mildew.

**Glass tabletops and mirrors** can develop stress fractures from rapid temperature changes.

**Mattresses** trap moisture and develop odor, stains, and structural breakdown without proper ventilation.

This is why a regular garage, an outdoor container, or an unregulated warehouse is a bad idea for furniture storage in the UAE. You need a controlled environment.

## Curtain Types We Install

### Standard Rod Curtains

### Curtain Track Systems

Ceiling or wall-mounted aluminum or plastic tracks with gliders. Common in modern villas and offices. We handle straight tracks, curved tracks, and bay window tracks.

### Motorized Curtains

Electrically operated curtains controlled by remote, wall switch, or smart home system. We install the track, the motor unit, and connect the wiring. Popular in high-end villas and smart homes across Dubai.

### Ceiling-Mounted Curtains

Used when there is no space above the window frame for a traditional rod, or for a floor-to-ceiling look. Requires drilling into the ceiling slab, which is different from wall mounting.

### Gypsum Board Mounting

Many UAE apartments and villas have gypsum board (drywall) ceilings and walls. Standard screws pull right out of gypsum. We use specialized anchors and toggle bolts rated for the weight of curtain rods and tracks.

## Curtain Care Tips After Installation

Once your curtains are up, a little maintenance keeps them looking good and lasting longer:

**Vacuum curtains monthly.** Use a brush attachment on low suction to remove dust. This prevents buildup that dulls fabric and triggers allergies.

**Rotate blackout and sheer layers.** If you have a double system, open the blackout curtains during the day to reduce UV fading on the sheer layer.

**Check brackets every few months.** UAE construction settles over time, especially in newer buildings. Brackets can loosen. Tightening them takes two minutes and prevents a curtain rail from falling.

**Clean curtain tracks annually.** Dust builds up inside tracks and makes curtains harder to open and close. A damp cloth along the track once a year keeps them running smoothly.

**Address motorized system issues early.** If your motorized curtains start moving slower or stopping midway, the motor may need cleaning or recalibration. Call us before the problem gets worse.
